Why the Inland Empire

The Finance Challenges Specific to This Region

The Inland Empire's industries have financial profiles most generalist CFOs have never worked with. This is what we see most often.

Logistics & Fulfillment Economics

Ontario, Fontana, and the I-10/I-15 corridor host some of the largest fulfillment operations in the country. 3PL margins are razor-thin. Customer concentration is dangerous. A single large account shifting volume can break the P&L. CFO-level forecasting and contract analysis is what keeps these businesses profitable.

Manufacturing & Heavy Industry

Metal fabrication, food processing, building products, aerospace components. Inland Empire manufacturers face rising labor costs, energy prices, and material input volatility. Product-level profitability analysis and pricing discipline separate the winners from the ones who close.

Healthcare Systems & Provider Groups

Riverside, San Bernardino, and the High Desert serve millions of patients. Multi-payer reimbursement, value-based care contracts, physician comp structures, and multi-site operations all need CFO-level rigor. Healthcare accounting is its own discipline.

Agriculture, Wine & Food

Coachella Valley agriculture, Temecula wine country, Riverside citrus. Seasonal revenue, commodity exposure, labor cost cycles, and the working capital dynamics that crush under-capitalized ag operators. Fractional CFO services tuned to the crop and climate calendar.

Every engagement — a 3PL in Ontario, a Temecula winery, a Riverside specialty clinic — is built on the same four principles.

R

Redundant

Documented, cross-trained processes. No single points of failure. Your finance function runs whether any one person is there or not.

A

Agile

Automation-first. We build systems before we hire people. Technology handles the repetitive work so your team handles the thinking.

I

Intelligent

Your finance team operates as owner-operators, not scorekeepers. They don't just report what happened — they drive what happens next.

D

Local Expertise

Across Riverside and San Bernardino Counties

The Inland Empire is one of California's fastest-growing regions. We serve companies across its distinct economic sub-regions.

Ontario & I-10 Logistics Corridor

One of the densest logistics clusters in the country. Ecommerce fulfillment, cross-dock operations, last-mile delivery, cold chain. Companies here need CFO leadership that understands freight economics, 3PL contracts, and working capital timing.

Riverside & the UCR Corridor

Healthcare, higher education, and a growing professional services base. Riverside's economy is scaling faster than its infrastructure, which creates opportunity for operators who can build disciplined finance functions from the ground up.

Temecula Wine Country

Southern California's underrated wine region. Wineries, wedding venues, and agritourism operations that face the same cash conversion challenges as their Paso Robles and Napa counterparts — with a different customer base and margin profile.

San Bernardino & the High Desert

Manufacturing, construction, transportation, energy. The industrial heart of the region, with companies that range from $20M family-owned operations to $200M distribution platforms. Finance leadership here needs to translate operational reality into investor-grade numbers.
