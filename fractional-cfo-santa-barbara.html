The Challenge

Why Santa Barbara Companies Outgrow Their Finance Function

The same qualities that make Santa Barbara County a great place to build a business — diverse industries, seasonal demand, rapid growth corridors — also create financial complexity that spreadsheets and part-time bookkeepers can't handle.

🍷

Wine & DTC Club Economics

Your 2023 vintage looks great on paper. Club sign-ups are up. Tasting room revenue is tracking. So why is the line of credit maxed and harvest is six weeks out? Because your CPA isn't modeling the cash conversion cycle and your bookkeeper isn't flagging it. Wineries across Santa Ynez, Solvang, Buellton, and Lompoc hit this wall almost every year. A fractional CFO builds the 13-week cash forecast that tells you in June whether you'll make it to crush — and the club unit economics that tell you which tier actually makes money.

🏖

Hospitality & Tourism Seasonality

July and August cover the year. November through February bleed. Santa Barbara and Carpinteria hospitality operators know the pattern, but most don't model it tightly enough to know which months actually break even — and which locations are subsidizing the others. A fractional CFO builds the seasonal working capital plan, the multi-unit P&L, and the capex timing that keeps you solvent through the shoulder seasons without panic-cutting staff in October.

💻

Tech Corridor Reporting Gap

You raised a seed round on a deck. The Series A term sheet wants a data room. Santa Barbara and Goleta tech companies — SaaS, biotech, deep tech — routinely hit this moment with a bookkeeper producing monthly P&Ls and no one building the unit economics, cohort analysis, or burn model institutional investors expect. A fractional CFO builds the reporting layer that closes the round, without the cost of a full-time CFO you don't need until Series B.

🛒

Ecommerce & DTC Growth

MER is holding. Revenue is up. Profit is down. Every DTC founder in Santa Barbara County hits this point — usually between $5M and $20M — when ad costs, inventory working capital, and fulfillment complexity start eating the margin that used to be obvious. A fractional CFO builds the contribution margin model by SKU and channel, the inventory cash lock-up analysis, and the MER-to-cash bridge that tells you what's actually funding growth and what's just financing the next ad buy.

AI & Process Automation

The Scary Stuff Your Finance Team Isn't Ready For

AI, automation, and process redesign aren't future problems — they're happening now. Most Santa Barbara finance teams are behind.

🤖

AI-Powered Financial Analysis

AI is already transforming how finance teams forecast, detect anomalies, and analyze margins. If your team is still building spreadsheets manually, you're losing ground. We implement AI-assisted forecasting, automated variance analysis, and intelligent reporting that turns raw data into decisions — not just dashboards.

⚙

Accounts Payable & Receivable Automation

Manual AP/AR processes are the single biggest time sink in mid-market finance. We automate invoice processing, payment matching, collections workflows, and approval routing — cutting cycle times by 60-80% and eliminating the human errors that create month-end reconciliation nightmares.

📊

Close Process Automation

If your monthly close still involves someone manually pulling reports from six different systems, you have a process problem masquerading as a people problem. We automate journal entries, intercompany eliminations, reconciliations, and reporting packages — taking the close from 15 days to 5.

🚀

Real-Time Cash Flow Intelligence

Spreadsheet-based cash forecasting is always stale by the time you look at it. We build automated cash flow models that pull live data from your bank accounts, AR aging, AP schedules, and payroll systems — giving you 13-week rolling visibility that updates daily, not monthly.
