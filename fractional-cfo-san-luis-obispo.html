Why SLO County

Finance Challenges Unique to San Luis Obispo

SLO County businesses face financial complexity that generic advisory firms don't understand. These are the specific pressures that demand real CFO leadership.

🍷

Paso Wine & Cash Conversion

Your 2023 vintage looks great on paper. Club sign-ups are up. Tasting room revenue is tracking. So why is the line of credit maxed and harvest is six weeks out? Because your CPA isn't modeling the cash conversion cycle and your bookkeeper isn't flagging it. Paso Robles and Templeton wineries hit this wall almost every year. A fractional CFO builds the 13-week cash forecast that tells you in June whether you'll make it to crush — and the club unit economics that tell you which tier actually makes money.

🏨

Hospitality Groups & Multi-Unit Economics

One location is a business. Three locations is a portfolio — and most hospitality groups in SLO and Paso hit the second phase without the reporting to match. Which location is subsidizing the others? Which month breaks even? When do you need to raise prices versus cut hours? A fractional CFO builds the multi-unit P&L, the labor-to-revenue model, and the seasonal working capital plan that keeps the group solvent through the shoulder seasons.

💻

SLO Tech & SaaS Scale-Up

You raised a seed round on a deck. The Series A term sheet wants a data room. SLO-based tech companies — SaaS, deep tech, hardware, Cal Poly spin-outs — routinely hit this moment with a bookkeeper producing monthly P&Ls and no one building unit economics, cohort analysis, or the burn model institutional investors expect. A fractional CFO builds the reporting layer that closes the round, without the cost of a full-time CFO you don't need until Series B.

🛒

Ecommerce & DTC Growth

MER is holding. Revenue is up. Profit is down. Every DTC founder hits this point — usually between $5M and $20M — when ad costs, inventory working capital, and fulfillment complexity start eating the margin that used to be obvious. A fractional CFO builds the contribution margin model by SKU and channel, the inventory cash lock-up analysis, and the MER-to-cash bridge that tells you what's actually funding growth and what's just financing the next ad buy.

Our Framework

The RAID Operating System

Every engagement — whether it's a Paso Robles winery or a SLO-based SaaS company — is built on the same four principles that transform finance from a bottleneck into a competitive advantage.

R

Redundant

Documented, cross-trained processes. No single points of failure. Your finance function runs whether any one person is there or not — critical for seasonal businesses with lean teams.

A

Agile

Automation-first. We build systems before we hire people. For businesses managing seasonal swings and growth spikes, agile finance means decisions happen in days, not weeks.

I

Intelligent

Your finance team operates as owner-operators, not scorekeepers. They don't just report what happened — they drive what happens next, whether that's a new vineyard acquisition or a product launch.

D

Data-Driven

Every team member owns 3-5 KPIs tied to real business levers. Real-time visibility into cash, margins, and the metrics that matter — not last month's numbers delivered three weeks late.

AI & Process Automation

The Technology Shift Your Finance Team Can't Ignore

AI and automation are rewriting the rules of finance operations. SLO County businesses that adapt will scale faster — those that don't will fall behind.

🤖

AI-Driven Forecasting

For wine, hospitality, and DTC businesses, forecasting is make-or-break. AI-powered models incorporate weather data, booking trends, ad spend signals, and historical patterns to produce cash flow and revenue forecasts that are dramatically more accurate than any spreadsheet. We implement these tools so your team acts on intelligence, not intuition.

⚙

End-to-End AP/AR Automation

Manual invoice processing, check runs, and collections calls eat 30+ hours per week at most mid-market companies. We automate the entire cycle — OCR invoice capture, three-way matching, automated approval routing, payment scheduling, and collections workflows — so your team focuses on analysis, not data entry.

📊

Automated Financial Close

The 15-day close is a relic. We implement automated reconciliations, scheduled journal entries, and pre-built reporting packages that close the books in 5 days or less. For companies with seasonal complexity — wineries managing barrel inventory, hotels managing room-night accruals — automation handles the repetitive work so your team handles the judgment calls.
